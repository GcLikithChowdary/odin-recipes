Egg Fried Rice

Description

Egg fried rice is a simple, versatile, and comforting dish that is a staple in many cuisines, particularly in Asian cooking. It is made by stir-frying cooked rice with scrambled eggs, vegetables, and seasonings, resulting in a flavorful and satisfying meal. The dish is known for its quick preparation and ability to use up leftover rice, making it a practical choice for busy weeknights or a quick lunch. The combination of fluffy rice, tender eggs, and crisp vegetables creates a delightful texture, while soy sauce, sesame oil, and other seasonings add depth and umami.

What makes egg fried rice so popular is its adaptability. It can be customized with a variety of ingredients, such as chicken, shrimp, or tofu, and enhanced with vegetables like peas, carrots, and bell peppers. The key to perfect egg fried rice lies in using cold, day-old rice, which prevents the dish from becoming mushy. Whether enjoyed as a main course or a side dish, egg fried rice is a timeless classic that is both easy to make and universally loved.

Incredients

  • 3 cups cooked and cooled rice (preferably day-old)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., peas, carrots, corn)
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Beat the eggs in a bowl, then pour them into the skillet and scramble until fully cooked. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil to the skillet and saut√© the minced garlic until fragrant.
  4. Add the mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until tender.
  5. Add the cooked rice to the skillet, breaking up any clumps with a spatula.
  6. Stir in the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and sesame oil, mixing well to coat the rice evenly.
  7. Add the scrambled eggs back to the skillet and gently fold them into the rice.
  8. Toss in the chopped green onions and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, until everything is heated through.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious egg fried rice!
