Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that has become a beloved comfort food worldwide. It consists of multiple layers of wide, flat pasta sheets alternated with rich fillings, typically including a savory meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. The meat sauce, often made with ground beef or pork, is simmered with tomatoes, garlic, onions, and herbs like basil and oregano, creating a robust and flavorful base. Between the layers, a blend of ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheeses adds a creamy, gooey texture, while the béchamel sauce provides a smooth, velvety richness. Once assembled, the lasagna is baked until the top is golden and bubbling, resulting in a hearty, indulgent dish that is perfect for sharing.

What makes lasagna so versatile is its adaptability to various dietary preferences and regional variations. Vegetarian versions might include layers of roasted vegetables, spinach, or mushrooms, while seafood lasagna incorporates shrimp or salmon for a unique twist. In some regions of Italy, lasagna is made without tomatoes, relying instead on a rich ragu and béchamel. Gluten-free and low-carb alternatives use zucchini or eggplant slices in place of pasta, catering to modern dietary needs. Whether served as a family dinner, at gatherings, or as a leftover treat, lasagna's layers of flavor and texture make it a timeless favorite that continues to evolve while staying true to its comforting roots.

Incredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups béchamel sauce (optional, or substitute with extra tomato sauce)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, brown ground beef or sausage with chopped onions and minced garlic.
  4. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper to the meat, then simmer for 20-30 minutes.
  5. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, egg, chopped parsley, salt, and pepper until combined.
  6. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  7. Place a layer of cooked lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  8. Spread half of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
  9. Add a layer of meat sauce over the ricotta.
  10. Sprinkle mozzarella and Parmesan cheese over the meat sauce.
  11. Repeat the layers: noodles, ricotta, meat sauce, and cheese.
  12. Finish with a final layer of noodles, meat sauce, and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan.
  13. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  14. Remove the foil and bake for another 20-25 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  15. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.
  16. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley and enjoy!
